Abstract

Automated analysis of spinal radiographs is critical to early diagnosis but is currently limited by severe class imbalance and the visual subtlety of lesions. This work introduces DERNet‚Äîan ensemble of DenseNet121, EfficientNetV2-S, and ResNet50‚Äîfor highly sensitive clas- sification, coupled with a YOLO11-L model for precise lesion detection. Our proposed method utilizes architectural diversity to overcome the sensitivity-specificity trade-off, along with a CSP-Darknet backbone to effectively capture the fine features of minute pathologies. The proposed framework is validated on the VinDr-SpineXR benchmark, achieving AUROC of 90.67¬±0.31% and detection mAP@0.5 of 41.2¬±0.3%. In addition to the technical contributions, the authors address the transla- tional challenge with the development of a functional web-based interface that can process various image formats, thereby facilitating the practical application of the proposed framework.

Proposed Framework: DERNet Dual-Stage Architecture

üî¥ The Challenge

  • Severe class imbalance in spine X-ray datasets (46.9:1 ratio - Osteophytes vs. Vertebral collapse)
  • Small object detection challenges with mean object areas of ~8,800 pixels¬≤
  • Need for both accurate classification and precise localization of spinal pathologies
  • Limited clinical trust due to lack of explainability and visualization in existing AI models

üü° Clinical Motivation

  • Enable automated triage of spine X-rays to prioritize abnormal cases requiring urgent review
  • Provide precise localization of 7 pathological findings: Osteophytes, Surgical implant, Spondylolisthesis, Foraminal stenosis, Disc space narrowing, Vertebral collapse, and Other lesions
  • Support radiologists with visual explanations through bounding box annotations
  • Reduce diagnostic time while maintaining high sensitivity (98.21%) to minimize false negatives

üü¢ DERNet Solution

  • Stage 1 - Classification Ensemble: Combines DenseNet-121 (8M params), EfficientNetV2-S (21.5M params), and ResNet-50 (25.6M params) with weighted fusion (0.38:0.36:0.26) and Test-Time Augmentation
  • Stage 2 - YOLO11-l Detection: Real-time object detection with CSPDarknet backbone, PANet neck, and decoupled heads for accurate lesion localization on abnormal cases
  • Dataset: VinDr-SpineXR with 8,389 training images, using Copy-Paste augmentation and Focal Loss to address class imbalance
  • Optimization: AdamW optimizer with cosine annealing, achieving AUROC 0.9641 for classification and mAP@50-95 of 0.511 for detection

Future Directions

Prospective research avenues to enhance DERNet's clinical impact and deployment.

  • ‚û§ Foundation Model Pre-training: Develop self-supervised learning frameworks on millions of unlabeled spine X-rays to create a universal spine pathology foundation model, enabling zero-shot transfer learning to rare diseases and low-resource clinical settings with minimal fine-tuning data.
  • ‚û§ 3D Spine Reconstruction from 2D X-rays: Leverage neural radiance fields (NeRF) and diffusion models to reconstruct 3D spinal anatomy from single or dual-view 2D radiographs, enabling volumetric analysis and surgical planning without requiring expensive CT/MRI scans.
  • ‚û§ Multi-Modal Clinical Integration: Fuse radiographic findings with electronic health records (clinical notes, lab results, patient demographics, prior imaging) using large language models and cross-attention mechanisms to provide holistic diagnostic predictions and personalized treatment recommendations.
  • ‚û§ Federated Learning Framework: Implement privacy-preserving collaborative learning across international hospital networks without centralizing patient data, using differential privacy and secure multi-party computation to overcome data sharing barriers while improving model generalization globally.
